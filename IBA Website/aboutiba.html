About IBA

In 1951, the International Bartenders Association was established in Torquay, UK on the 24 February by 7 founding members (guilds), Denmark, France, Italy, Netherlands, Sweden, Switzerland and the United Kingdom. The first President was Mr Bill Tarling from UKBG, followed George Baker the next year, then by George Sieve of Switzerland, Pietro Grandi of Italy, and Kurt Sorensen of Denmark.

From 1963 to 1976 (14 years) the presidency was in Italy held by Mr Angelo Zola. Angelo Zola was also one of the founding members at the round table meeting for the inception of the IBA.

The IBA flourished and by 1976 it had recruited 23 new members, making the IBA with a membership of 30 countries. To-date the IBA membership stands at 64 with 3 observing countries.

Message from the President

The International Bartenders’ Association is the crossroad of exchange between new and old generations of professionals, it is the meeting point of all professional optics. It is the splendid gym where competition is exercised in view of a single common goal: our professional identity.

I would not want to fall into the obvious, but here is where our asociative “spirit” emerges: robust in its professional solidity, and full-bodied for everyone who takes part in it, every year, with new and consolidated experiences.

The commitment, and devotion that is given by our 64 member countries, underline how important and strong our association is.

A special recognition to our Partners, that despise the current socio-economic situation continue supporting our causes and working for the benefit of the world’s bartending community.

What We Are

The IBA is a community of bartender associations engaging in sharing knowledge and innovations. We give our community equal opportunities for personal and career advancement. The IBA is a global non-profit organization of passionate individuals who cherish the traditions and heritage of our trade.



What We Do

The IBA connects the beverage industry professionals together. We raise the high standards of service and bartending skills through our partnerships, Academy, resources and international competitions. These activities bring our diverse family of national associations together.
